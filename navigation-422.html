U-M Campus Navigation User Research

Timeline: 8 weeks

Role: UX Researcher

Tools: Miro, G Suite

Project description: We recieved the following brief: "U-M would like to create a campus navigation app to help students. Research question: What are the biggest challenges that students face in getting around campus and finding what they are looking for?" We both prepared and conducted experience research to answer this question and determine what they are looking for for use in an application.

Overview

This project consisted of five primary phases. First, we constructed an interview guide and conducted interviews with current U-M students about their navigational experiences. Next, we combed through our data and identified common themes and patterns across our sample population. Then, we used these findings to construct a survey which we then sent out to a larger population of students. After, we used these survey results in conjunction with our interview data to further understand the behaviors, attitudes, needs, and desires of our target audience. Finally, we developed a user persona and accompanying scenarios to encapsulate the mindset of our target user.

Interview Stage

For the first two weeks of this project, we identified research questions and built an interview guide. Next we conducted user interviews to identify the needs, desires, and current pain points of Michigan students when navigating campus.

Data Analysis

After conducting interviews, my team coded our notes and transcripts to identify recurring themes. After establishing the main patterns, we utilized affinity diagramming to confirm key findings in the data and establish their significance to our research.

Developing Personas & Scenarios

Based on key findings from the user interviews we conducted, we developed user personas we believed represented the target user of our product and wrote cooresponding scenarios to articulate the unique challenges that U-M students face with navigation.

Key Takeaways

The primary lesson I learned from the prep and interview stages of this project is the importance of being unbiased and thorough. Sometimes subjects need a bit of push to give us the data we need, so asking leading questions and easing them into an interview are very important. But it is also critical to ask questions in a non-judgemental and impartial manner in order to recieve accurate and actionable information.

I would say that the data analysis and survey constructing phases of this project were the biggest learning experiences for me. Through affinity diagramming, we learned how to make effective use of qualitative data to uncover recurring themes. Then, we utilized these patterns to construct a simple survey which allowed us to recieve more quantitative information, which was easier to analyze and understand.

I also learned a lot through the persona and scenario development phase of this project. Creating specific identities allowed me to see how our research would be practically applied, and how effective this use would be. From here, it became easy to understand the strengths and shortcomings of both the existing interface, and also our proposed design.

Next Steps

If we were to actually proceed with development of this application, there are a few key features our research identified as crucial for our target audience.

  • More accurate information!
    • As it exists, the MagicBus application often lags and is otherwise misleading. Developing an application with real-time information would better allow students to plan their travel in advance.
  • Advanced and complex travel planning
    • Many students expressed frustration at the inability to plot out their day's travel in advance. We would like to implement a feature that allows students to integrate transportation times and modes into their schedule to ensure a timely appearance at important activities.
  • Indoor campus maps and resources
    • We also discovered through this process that many students feel the existing resources for finding classrooms and other indoor resources are insufficient. We would like to incorporate building maps and guides into this application for more efficient travel once indoors.
