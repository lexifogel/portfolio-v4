Craigslist: Identifying Usability Issues

Project description: This project challenged my team to diagnose all observable usability problems with Craigslist. Then, we classified these issues and prioritied which should be fixed first to help define clear next steps for the company.

Problem: Major usability problems prevent Craigslist from seeing its potential success

Goal: Evaluate problems for severity and establish a plan of action for bettering customers' experiences on the site

Process: Heuristic Evaluation

Process

The first step of this evaluation was to perform a variety of actions on the site and identify any usability issues we noticed. Next, we went through these problems one by one and determined which of Nielson's Heuristics they represented. Finally, we assigned each problem a severity rating according to this scale to determine in what order they should be approached in the redesign process.

The issues were ranked from most severe to least severe, according to the following scale:

  1. Cosmetic problem
  2. Minor usability problem
  3. Major usability problem; important to fix
  4. Usability catastrophe; imperative to fix

Key Findings from Heuristic Evaluation

Usability issue #1: Location limitations

Craigslist does not remember where I chose my location to be last, so I have to go through the entire list and find it. Also, it should indicate that it doesn’t know where you are (rather than guessing), and give users the option to find their region manually.

  • Heuristic(s) violated: Recognition rather than recall, Visibility of system status
  • Severity rating: 3

Figure 1: Wrong location (I'm in Ann Arbor!)

On the Craigslist world map page there is no search bar capability to easily find your location; they give you a premade list of locations.

  • Heuristic(s) violated: User control and freedom
  • Severity rating: 2

Figure 2: Location list

Usability issue #2: Title Shorthands

Many category names are shortened in non-intuitive ways (for example: health > hlth, business development > biz dev, hospitality > hosp, translation > tran)

Users cannot efficiently navigate to categories if they don’t understand how they are labeled! If I was looking for an editorial or translator job, “write/ed/tran” would not stand out to me as the correct category (if not for write”, I wouldn’t guess that one at all).

  • Heuristic(s) violated: Consistency and standards
  • Severity rating: 3

Figure 3: Poor shorthands

Usability issue #3: Page style

It is hard to know where to go when there are so many words on the main page distracting the user. The user's attention is not on one place when they open Craigslist's site. It makes it stressful to use as a first-time user to and difficult to understand how to navigate through. Also, the limited variety of colors utilized on the site amplifies this effect.

  • Heuristic(s) violated: Aesthetics and minimalist design
  • Severity rating: 3

Minimal capitalization is used on the entire site. Capitalization is used to indicate proper nouns, categories or sections, etc in most interfaces. Since Craigslist does not follow these conventions, items seem to blend together and it is hard to recognize what’s most important.

  • Heuristic(s) violated: Match between system and real world
  • Severity rating: 2

Limited styling is applied to the website, and it looks outdated! Nobody is going to choose a site that looks like this when there are alternatives which do the same thing, but look modern & so much better.

  • Heuristic(s) violated: Aesthetics and minimalist design
  • Severity rating: 2

Figure 4: Ugly page

Usability issue #4: No visible login status

The website has a “my account” page, which indicates that users should log in or create an account, but the main page itself does not show if a user is logged in or how to do so. If a user is actually trying to use Craiglist (not just browse the site like we are), it would be frustrating to do an action (post, comment, etc – which I’m surprised it even lets you do without being logged in) just to realize afterwards that you did it anonymously and not from your account.

  • Heuristic(s) violated: Visibility of system status
  • Severity rating: 3

Figure 5: Hidden login status -- found under "my account"

Usability issue #5: Inefficient reply mechanism

The “reply” button on a posting just leads to another button (“show email”), which still doesn’t take you to a page where you can send an email but rather gives a link/address that emails should be sent to. When people hit the “reply” button and are not immediately provided a form to fill out which will submit their response, they are 1) confused (because that’s how most reply buttons work) and 2) irritated (because the reply process is so inefficient).

  • Heuristic(s) violated: Flexibility and efficiency of use, Consistency and standards
  • Severity rating: 3

Results Anaysis

I would say that Craigslist’s strengths lie mostly in the recognition rather than recall and help and documentation areas. One thing that’s great about Craigslist is that it consistently shows not just the user’s location, but the path of actions that they took to get to the current page; for example, if I’m on the pets page, the top navigation will say “ann arbor > community > pets”, aka showing the location, category, and relevant subcategory. Another cool thing that Craigslist does is remember what pages the user has already visited–as is common in most browsers, it highlights visited pages (links) in purple instead of the usual blue.

Some weaknesses of Craigslist have to do with the visuals of the site. The page is very plain/minimally styled, and almost all text is in all lowercase (even when it shouldn’t be). This can be problematic because users are used to proper nouns, category names, and beginnings of sentences or list items being capitalized–this makes them stand out, and is also the normal convention, so not being that way can be confusing to new users.

Next Steps

If Craigslist were to implement this feedback and make substantial design changes to their site, this is the order in which we'd recommend:

  • Page style
    • As it exists, the Craigslist site is not just visually unappealing, but functionally confusing. We would suggest re-styling the page in a more modern style, and using a greater variety of colors (or just different ones than the browser default) to increase contrast between sections and highlight different elements of the page. Additionally, it would be helpful to follow proper capitalization conventions to make the comprehension process more natural for users. I would say that changing the styling of the site should be one of the top priorities when redesigning -- since the page looks outdated and there are more functional and modern alternatives, it is very easy for users to choose something like Facebook marketplace over Craigslist.
  • Memory limitations
    • Another high priority should be the website’s memory capabilities –- since it often forgets the user’s location and has no ability to suggest actions based on prior interactions (shortcuts), users might be frustrated having to re-enter information each time they engage.
  • Visibility of login status
    • The main/home page does not indicate if the current user is logged in, or give any insight on how to do so. If a user is trying to use Craiglist to buy or sell products or services (not just browse the site like we did), it would be take actions which they later realize were not affiliated with their profile.
