Blaze Pizza: Usability Testing

Project description: With this project, a team and I built and conducted user tests and collected and analyzed both quantitative and qualitative data to assess users' current experience with the Blaze Pizza website and make suggestions regarding the most effective changes that could be made.

Problem: Various usability problems with their website prevent Blaze Pizza from seeing maximum potential revenue

Goal: Use data to identify the most detrimental issues on Blaze Pizza's website and develop a strategy for its redesign

Timeline: 2 weeks

Test Development

Before we could collect any data, we first had to construct a test to measure user's current interactions with Blaze Pizza's website. We wrote tasks which required users to engage with most aspects of the site, even those users might not frequently visit, and recorded either time to complete the task (for performance-focused questions) or rating on a Likert satisfaction scale (for preference-based tasks).

Figure 1: Test plan setup

Data Collection

We utilized remote, unmoderated usability testing for this assignment. We conducted our tests through the online platform UserTesting.com, which provides researchers an audio and screen recording of each subject's testing session in addition to the responses the participants provide. We were able to review these videos to better understand the reasoning behind subjects' actions and their opinions on engagement with various elements of the site.

My team consisted of five members, so different subgroups of us conducted identical tests on subjects using a variety of devices; two of us conducted mobile tests, one for tablets, and another two for computers. Because we were using the free version of the UserTesting platform, we recieved three subjects for each test, or a total of 15 users overall.

My research specifically focused on the mobile version of the website. Although my test alone only measured the experiences of three different individuals, even that was enough to make some specific, justified suggestions on what elements of the webpage require alterations.

Analysis

As mentioned previously, we included two different types of tasks in our usability assessment: performance-based tasks, measured with metrics such as completion time or number of errors, and preference-focused tasks, analyzed with use of a Likert scale. For the performance tasks in our test, the quantitative measure we tracked was task completion time. With the preference ones, we found the median value of satisfaction ratings submitted by the participants.

Key Takeaways

Overall, users seemed to like the current Blaze pizza website. It allows customers a lot of freedom; for example, when adding a pizza to the cart, all individual ingredients can be easily changed with one click.

Despite this success, there are also many elements of the site which have proven difficult and frustrating to mobile users. The most significant of these are discussed below.

Next Steps

If Blaze Pizza were to utilize our research to guide a website update or redesign, these are the key suggestions I would offer:

  • Redesign the merch shop
    • While most of Blaze’s website is just fine on mobile devices, the external merchandise store is irritatingly incompatible. Although I don’t expect that many Blaze customers visit this specific page (which is probably why it hasn’t been fixed), those that do will be immediately deterred by the difficulty of navigating it. Users complained about a few specific problems within this page: it is hard to find the shipping price calculator, there is no intuitive backtracking strategy, and the site doesn’t fit on the screen. Since this is not a critical page on the site, users will just choose not to access it rather than switching to a different device or making do with what they're using.
  • Add a link to the nutrition calculator from the cart
    • All three of my participants suggested this, and it’s one of the easiest fixes I see potentially being made to the site. Adding a quick “See nutrition info!” button which would allow users to import their cart item(s) directly to the nutrition calculator would make that element of the site much easier to use, thus significantly increasing interaction with it.
  • Explain the loyalty program on the loyalty page
    • Two of my three subjects reported being unable to find much information on the loyalty program, and the one that did really had to dig deep into the site (info was on the FAQs page) to find it. If users understand the details of the program and the benefits they would get, I believe many more would sign up and thus return to Blaze more often.
